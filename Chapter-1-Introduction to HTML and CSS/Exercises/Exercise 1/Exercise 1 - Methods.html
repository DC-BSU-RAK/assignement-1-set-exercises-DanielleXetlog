METHOD

  1. Put 2 cups of sushi rice in a large bowl and rinse it by filling it with cold water and gently rinse the rice with your hands. Pour the cloudy water and repeat it this 4–5 times until the cloudy water is gone.
  2. When the cloudy water is gone, it's time to drain it with a fine-mesh strainer for 10–15 minutes.
  3. After rinsing the rice, it's time to cook it by adding the rice and 2 ½ cups of water to the rice cooker and wait for it to cook for 30–40 minutes.
  4. While the rice is cooking, mix ⅓ cup rice vinegar, 2 tablespoons sugar, and 1 teaspoon salt in a small saucepan. Heat it up with low heat and gently stir is until the sugar and salt dissolve, after that set it aside to cool down.
  5. Next is transferring the cooked rice to a large bowl and pour vinegar mixture evenly over the rice. Gently fold the rice and mix the seasoning using a wooden spoon. Use a cutting motion than stirring to avoid mashing the grains.
  6. Have a cold temperature room while folding, giving it a glossy look.
  7. Put a cloth so it'll be moist and ready to use.
  1. Next is the fish, cut the fish against the grain into thin pieces and regular slices of 2–3 inches long, 1 wide and ¼ inch thick.
  2. For shrimp, boil it for 1–2 minutes, then split the lengthwise and flatten it.
  3. Keep sliced fish cold by putting it on a plate over ice.
  1. Now for the shaping, fill up the small bowl with water with a tiny bit of vinegar. This avoids rice sticking on to your hands.
  2. Have rice with seasoning and sliced fish with a small amount of wasabi close to you. Get 1–2 tablespoons of sushi rice and gently put the rice between your palm and fingers to create an oblong shape of about 2 inches long and 1 inch wide. After that, use your thumb to create an indent on the bottom and middle finger for the top.
  3. Dab a wasabi if interest on the underside of the fish slice with your finger to add flavor.
  4. Put the sliced fish on the rice ball and gently press it.
  1. Do the same method for each nigiri pieces and put it on a clean plate and place the fish side up.
  2. Put a small dish of soy sauce, a pile of pickled ginger and extra wasabi on the side.
